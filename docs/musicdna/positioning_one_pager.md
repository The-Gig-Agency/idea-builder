# MusicDNA — Positioning One-Pager

## The one sentence

We are not building a catalog of songs. We are building a catalog of revealing decisions.

## The problem with music personality tests

Most of them sound like horoscopes with a soundtrack.

"You're a dreamer. You value authenticity. You're an old soul."

Nobody learns anything. Nobody argues with it. Nobody remembers it an hour later.

Spotify measures what you listen to. Everyone else measures what you say you like. Both miss the interesting part: **what you choose when you can't have both.**

## What we do differently

MusicDNA is a structured conversation with an AI music critic. It hands you forced pairwise choices between two songs you probably both love — `Ceremony` or `Dreaming of Me`, `Temptation` or `This Charming Man` — and watches which one you sacrifice.

Under the hood, every read is earned:

1. **Observations** — what you chose, what you rejected.
2. **Patterns** — the tradeoffs those choices keep making.
3. **Counter-hypotheses** — the boring alternative explanations (you just like New Order, you just like early-80s production).
4. **Evidence threshold** — no claim ships until enough choices support it.
5. **Narrative** — a critic voice writes the read, and it can only say things the evidence allows.

We describe the choice, not the chooser. "You consistently choose songs that build toward emotional release" instead of "you value hope." Every read is something you can push back on — which is exactly why the good ones land.

## The inverted stack

Everyone else starts from songs and works up. We start from identity and work down.

```text
IDENTITY
    ^
TASTE
    ^
MATCHUPS
    ^
SONGS
```

Ask the questions in this order:

1. What are we trying to learn about a person?
2. Which dimensions reveal that?
3. Which matchup reveals those dimensions?
4. Which songs create that matchup?

## What the product feels like

Not this:

> Your selections indicate a preference for atmospheric compositions characterized by immersive sonic environments.

This:

> You keep choosing songs that move.
> Not fast. Just forward.
> What happens if I throw you something that stands still?

Or:

> Cracked voice over the perfect take. Every time.
> You don't want the song fixed. You want it bleeding.
> Let's see if that holds.

## What we're not

- Not Spotify. We don't recommend what to play next.
- Not Myers-Briggs. We don't put you in a bucket.
- Not astrology. Every read carries its evidence.
- Not a music-crit encyclopedia. The song is not the point. You are.

## The moat

The catalog is table stakes. The moat is the **taste graph** — millions of comparative decisions from real listeners, each one a small revealing tradeoff. That data doesn't exist anywhere else, because nobody else is asking the question this way.

The AI can change. The prose can change. The graph compounds.

## Where we are

MVP. Alternative lane first, other subcultures next. The engine is stable enough to instrument and honest enough to say "I'm not sure yet." The remaining work is less about intelligence and more about making the first ninety seconds feel obvious to someone who has never thought about music this way.

If we consistently produce two or three moments per session where a user says *"I've never thought about it that way,"* the product works — not because it's perfectly accurate, but because it's meaningfully revealing.
